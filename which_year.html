My Role

Sole UX lead—from initial concept through launch and ongoing iterations—responsible for user research, interaction design, visual design, front-end implementation, and measuring success.

Project Genesis

I set out to create a simple daily habit that blends quick visual intuition with a dash of history. Inspired by Wordle's shareable ritual and nostalgia for old photographs, I sketched the core loop on paper:

  • See a photo
  • Guess its year (four-digit input + optional digit reveals)
  • Get instant feedback (points + context)
  • Share results

Key early design principles:

  • Mobile-first: Full-screen imagery, thumb-friendly controls.
  • Guided input: A slider plus tap-to-reveal individual digits.
  • Contextual captions: Brief "hint" before guessing, rich context after.
  • Dark, distraction-free UI: Focus on the photo.

Phase 1: MVP & Early Traction

Launch (April 2025): A lean HTML/CSS/JS prototype.

Organic word-of-mouth: Pitched on Reddit's r/photography, shared on Hacker News & YC forums.

Initial metrics:

  • ~1 min average challenge time
  • 35% next-day retention
  • 2 K total plays within the first week

Phase 2: Listening & Opportunity Framing

Over the next two weeks, I harvested ~150 comments from social feeds, forums, and DMs and affinity-mapped them into three themes:

"How good is my score?"

"Cool photo, but is 3 750 points any good?" —New player on Discord

"I wish I could play more than five days."

"Love this—can I revisit yesterday's?" —Reddit comment

"When does the next challenge drop?"

"I'm in Tokyo—when does midnight ET reset happen?" —YC thread

I paired these themes with two quantitative signals: average session length (stuck near 1 min) and next-day retention (stuck near 35%).

Feature Deep Dives

Data Visualization

Before: Points only.

After: Mini bell-curve after each guess and final distribution chart.

Result: Average time-on-challenge rose 30% as players compared their performance.

Archive

Why: Power users wanted more than five plays.

What: A calendar UI showing past days; clicking opens a fully playable challenge.

Result: 40% of daily users now explore the Archive.

Countdown Timer

Why: Global confusion around ET reset.

What: Persistent timer at top of screen, recalculated client-side.

Result: Support tickets and "When's next?" forum posts dropped by 75%.

Shareable Results

Why: Many players found the big "X" icon demotivating.

What: Swap out for a neutral dot beyond a 10-year miss; update share text to prioritize "Top X%" and average years off.

Result: Shares increased 20%, with more positive sentiment on social feeds.
